Offer & Counteroffer Templates

📗 Complete Course⏱ 32 min read🏠 Module 12 of 12

Templates save time, reduce errors, and ensure you don't miss anything important under the pressure of a live negotiation. This module walks you through how purchase offer forms work, provides a line-by-line explanation of a standard offer, and gives you ready-to-adapt templates for the most common FSBO scenarios: the single offer, the counteroffer, the multiple-offer response, the inspection repair request response, and the rejection letter. These templates are starting points — always have a real estate attorney review documents before signing in your specific transaction.

How Purchase Offer Forms Work

When a buyer's agent submits an offer, they typically use their state's standard residential purchase agreement — the same form we discussed in Module 5. When an unrepresented buyer (one without an agent) submits an offer to an FSBO seller, they may:

  • Use the state standard form if they obtained it themselves
  • Submit a letter of intent that outlines terms, which you then formalize on the standard form
  • Ask you for a contract template — in which case you can use your state's standard form or a form your real estate attorney provides

Do not use online "generic" purchase contract templates that aren't specific to your state. Real estate contracts are heavily regulated by state law, and a non-compliant form creates legal risk. Use your state's standard form — available from your state's real estate commission or a local real estate attorney.

Line-by-Line: Reading a Standard Offer

Here are the key fields you'll encounter in any standard residential purchase offer and what to look for in each:

FieldWhat to Look ForRed Flags
Purchase PriceThe offered amountMore than 5–7% below list price without justification
Earnest MoneyAmount and deposit deadlineLess than 1% of purchase price; extended deposit deadline
Financing TypeConventional, FHA, VA, CashFHA/VA with low down payment on a home with condition issues
Down Payment %Buyer's equity in the dealUnder 5% — buyer is more likely to walk if issues arise
Loan AmountShould match purchase price minus down paymentInconsistency between purchase price, down payment, and loan amount
Closing DateProposed closing timelineLess than 25 days for financed offer (typically not achievable)
Inspection ContingencyNumber of days for inspectionMore than 14 days is unusual; no inspection contingency is favorable to you
Financing ContingencyNumber of days to secure loanMore than 30 days; vague language about loan terms
Appraisal ContingencyWaived or in placeIn place at a price significantly above your comp range
Closing Costs RequestedSeller contribution to buyer's costsMore than 3% of purchase price in a normal market
Personal PropertyWhat the buyer expects includedItems not normally included (furniture, décor)

Template 1: Acceptance Letter (Full Price, No Changes)

When you receive an offer that meets your terms and you want to accept it as written:

📋 ACCEPTANCE LETTER TEMPLATE

Date: [DATE]

To: [BUYER NAME] / [BUYER'S AGENT NAME if applicable]

Re: Acceptance of Purchase Offer — [PROPERTY ADDRESS]

Dear [Buyer Name],

We are pleased to confirm our acceptance of your purchase offer dated [DATE] for the property located at [ADDRESS] at the offered purchase price of $[AMOUNT] under the terms as submitted.

Enclosed/attached is the fully executed Purchase and Sale Agreement. Please confirm receipt and ensure the earnest money deposit of $[AMOUNT] is delivered to [TITLE COMPANY NAME] by [DATE] per the contract terms.

We look forward to a smooth transaction and wish you well in your new home.

Sincerely,
[YOUR NAME]
Owner-Seller | [PROPERTY ADDRESS]
[YOUR PHONE] | [YOUR EMAIL]

Template 2: Counteroffer Letter

When you want to modify the buyer's offer on one or more terms:

📋 COUNTEROFFER TEMPLATE

Date: [DATE]

To: [BUYER NAME] / [BUYER'S AGENT NAME if applicable]

Re: Seller's Counteroffer — [PROPERTY ADDRESS]

Dear [Buyer Name],

Thank you for your offer dated [DATE] for the above-referenced property. After careful review, we respectfully submit the following counteroffer. All terms of your original offer remain in effect except as modified below:

1. Purchase Price: $[YOUR COUNTER PRICE] (modified from $[BUYER'S OFFERED PRICE])
2. Closing Date: [YOUR PREFERRED DATE] (modified from [BUYER'S PROPOSED DATE])
3. Earnest Money: $[YOUR REQUESTED AMOUNT] to be deposited within [X] business days of acceptance
4. Seller Closing Cost Contribution: [State your position — e.g., "Seller will contribute $0 toward buyer's closing costs" or "Seller will contribute up to $X"]\
5. [Any additional modifications]

This counteroffer expires at [TIME] on [DATE]. Please confirm your acceptance or response in writing prior to this deadline.

We remain committed to working toward a mutually beneficial agreement and look forward to your response.

Sincerely,
[YOUR NAME]
Owner-Seller | [PROPERTY ADDRESS]
[YOUR PHONE] | [YOUR EMAIL]

Template 3: Multiple Offer Notification

When you've received more than one offer and want to invite best-and-final submissions:

📋 MULTIPLE OFFER NOTIFICATION TEMPLATE

Date: [DATE]

To: [BUYER NAME] / [BUYER'S AGENT NAME]

Re: Multiple Offer Situation — [PROPERTY ADDRESS]

Dear [Buyer Name],

Thank you for your interest in [PROPERTY ADDRESS]. We want to inform you that we have received multiple purchase offers on this property.

We are requesting that all interested buyers submit their highest and best offer by [TIME] on [DATE]. Please ensure your best offer addresses: purchase price, earnest money, financing terms, contingencies, closing date, and any other terms important to your offer.

We will review all offers received by the deadline and respond to the selected buyer within [X] hours/business days.

We appreciate your continued interest and look forward to reviewing your best offer.

Sincerely,
[YOUR NAME]
Owner-Seller | [PROPERTY ADDRESS]

Template 4: Inspection Response — Accepting Requests

📋 INSPECTION RESPONSE — ACCEPTING TEMPLATE

Date: [DATE]

To: [BUYER NAME] / [BUYER'S AGENT NAME]

Re: Inspection Response — [PROPERTY ADDRESS]

Dear [Buyer Name],

Thank you for submitting your inspection response dated [DATE]. After careful review, we respond as follows:

Items We Agree To Address:
• [ITEM 1]: Seller will [repair using licensed contractor / provide closing credit of $X]
• [ITEM 2]: Seller will [repair using licensed contractor / provide closing credit of $X]

Items We Decline to Address:
• [ITEM 3]: [Brief, professional explanation — e.g., "This item was disclosed in our seller disclosure and is reflected in the purchase price.")
• [ITEM 4]: [e.g., "This is normal wear consistent with the age of the home and was visible during the buyer's inspection period.")

The above represents our complete response to the inspection request. We remain committed to closing on [CLOSING DATE] and look forward to your confirmation to proceed.

Sincerely,
[YOUR NAME]

Template 5: Rejection Letter

When you're declining an offer outright (no counteroffer):

📋 REJECTION LETTER TEMPLATE

Date: [DATE]

To: [BUYER NAME] / [BUYER'S AGENT NAME]

Re: Response to Purchase Offer — [PROPERTY ADDRESS]

Dear [Buyer Name],

Thank you for your offer dated [DATE] for [PROPERTY ADDRESS]. After careful consideration, we have decided to decline this offer at this time.

We appreciate your interest in our home and wish you the best in your search.

Sincerely,
[YOUR NAME]
Owner-Seller | [PROPERTY ADDRESS]

⚠️ Keep All Communications Professional and Brief

Rejection letters and counteroffers should never include emotional commentary, explanations of why you're insulted, or personal reactions to the offer. Keep all written communications factual, brief, and professional. You may encounter this buyer again — in a different market, at a different price — and bridges should never be burned unnecessarily.

The Post-Acceptance Checklist

After you've accepted an offer, send the buyer (or their agent) a confirmation package that includes:

  • Fully executed purchase agreement (signed by all parties)
  • Your seller disclosure form(s)
  • Lead paint disclosure (if applicable)
  • HOA documents (if applicable)
  • Title company contact information and escrow instructions
  • Showing schedule cancellation notice (the home is under contract — cancel any pending showings)
  • Your direct contact information for transaction coordination

🎯 Key Takeaways

  • Always use your state's standard purchase contract form — generic online templates create legal risk
  • Evaluate every offer across the full table: price, earnest money, financing type, contingencies, closing costs, and timeline
  • All counteroffers should be in writing, address all modifications simultaneously, and include an expiration deadline
  • Multiple-offer notification should go to all buyers simultaneously — never share one buyer's terms with another
  • Inspection responses should address each requested item explicitly — accept, counter with credit, or decline with brief professional language
  • Keep all written communications brief, professional, and emotion-free — they become part of the transaction record
